<h3 style="font-family:sans-serif; color:#555; font-weight:600;">
Piezo Transducer Placement Guidelines
</h3>

<p style="font-family:sans-serif; max-width:850px; line-height:1.6;">
This document defines a placement methodology for piezoelectric contact sensors based on structural vibration behavior and energy transfer efficiency in wooden percussion instruments.  
The goal is to maximize low-frequency response, transient clarity, and consistency of mechanical coupling.
</p>

<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #ddd; margin:18px 0;">

<h3 style="font-family:sans-serif; text-transform:uppercase; letter-spacing:0.05em; border-left:4px solid #000; padding-left:10px;">
Coupling Principles
</h3>

<p style="font-family:sans-serif; line-height:1.6; max-width:850px;">
Effective piezo transduction depends on mechanical energy transfer rather than post-processing.
</p>

<p style="font-family:sans-serif; line-height:1.6;">
<strong>Rigid Coupling:</strong> Sensor must be mechanically fixed for stable energy transfer.<br>
<strong>Structural Continuity:</strong> Avoid compliant interfaces that attenuate low frequencies.<br>
<strong>Full-Body Response:</strong> Piezo sensors respond to structural displacement, not surface vibration.
</p>

<h3 style="font-family:sans-serif; text-transform:uppercase; letter-spacing:0.05em; border-left:4px solid #000; padding-left:10px;">
Low-Frequency Behavior
</h3>

<p style="font-family:sans-serif; line-height:1.6; max-width:850px;">
Wooden instruments exhibit strong resonances typically in the 40–80 Hz range depending on geometry and material structure. Preserving these modes is essential for full-body response.
</p>

<h3 style="font-family:sans-serif; text-transform:uppercase; letter-spacing:0.05em; border-left:4px solid #000; padding-left:10px;">
Installation Requirements
</h3>

<p style="font-family:sans-serif;">
<strong>Mechanical:</strong> rigid coupling required, avoid soft interfaces unless explicitly designed.<br>
<strong>Validation:</strong> verify consistent transient response, avoid nodal cancellation zones, confirm low-frequency retention.
</p>

<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #ddd; margin:18px 0;">

<p style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:0.9rem; color:#444;">
Placement is a structural decision directly affecting transducer performance through mechanical energy transfer efficiency.
</p>
