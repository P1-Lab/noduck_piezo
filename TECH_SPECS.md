<h2>Status: Technical Specification (Draft)</h2>

<p>
This document serves as the geometric precursor to formal CAD outputs. It defines the mandatory physical boundaries for the Sovereign Capsule and constrains all downstream mechanical design.
</p>

<hr>

<h3>1. External Dimensions (Lipstick Form Factor)</h3>

<p>
The housing is defined as a cylindrical enclosure optimized for retrofitting into standard instrument cavities. Geometry is fixed and non-optional.
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Outside Diameter (OD):</strong> 38.0 mm (accommodates 35.0 mm piezo element with 1.5 mm wall clearance)</li>
  <li><strong>Total Height:</strong> 12.0 mm to 15.0 mm (low-profile mounting envelope)</li>
  <li><strong>Wall Thickness:</strong> 1.5 mm minimum (Zinc or Brass required for inertial stability)</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>2. Material Interface (Base Plate)</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Bottom Plate Thickness:</strong> 2.0 mm</li>
  <li><strong>Material Requirement:</strong> Bonded Baltic Birch or Mahogany interface layer</li>
  <li><strong>Function:</strong> Establishes wood-on-wood boundary coupling (Material Bridge)</li>
  <li><strong>Tolerance:</strong> ±0.05 mm for press-fit integrity</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>3. Internal Cavity</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Sensor Clearance:</strong> Internal diameter must allow 35 mm brass piezo to sit flush against base plate without edge constraint</li>
  <li><strong>Shielding:</strong> Fully enclosed metallic cavity required for electrical and environmental isolation</li>
  <li><strong>Exit Port:</strong> 3.0 mm lateral cable exit with integrated strain relief structure</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>4. Mass Requirements</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Target Housing Mass:</strong> &gt; 40 g</li>
</ul>

<p>
Increased mass is required to stabilize mechanical response and suppress high-frequency transient artifacts (“quack” suppression through inertia).
</p>

<hr>

<h3>Contributor Note</h3>

<p>
These specifications define the geometric and material constraints for generation of STEP or DWG outputs within the repository.
All derived CAD models must preserve these constraints without deviation.
</p>

<p>
The geometry is not aesthetic. It is a functional expression of the Architecture of Necessity.
</p>
