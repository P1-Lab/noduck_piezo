<h2>Status: Technical Specification (Draft)</h2>

<p>
This document defines the geometric precursor to formal CAD outputs. It establishes the physical constraints for the Sovereign Capsule housing and constrains all downstream mechanical design.
</p>

<hr>

<h3>1. External Dimensions (Cylindrical Housing Standard)</h3>

<p>
The housing is defined as a compact cylindrical enclosure designed for retrofit into standard instrument cavities. Geometry is fixed and non-optional.
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Outside Diameter (OD):</strong> 38.0 mm (provides clearance for 35.0 mm piezo element and structural wall thickness)</li>
  <li><strong>Total Height:</strong> 12.0 mm to 15.0 mm (low-profile mounting envelope)</li>
  <li><strong>Wall Thickness:</strong> 1.5 mm minimum (Zinc or Brass required for inertial stability)</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>2. Material Interface (Base Plate)</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Bottom Plate Thickness:</strong> 2.0 mm</li>
  <li><strong>Material Requirement:</strong> Baltic Birch or Mahogany interface layer</li>
  <li><strong>Function:</strong> Establishes wood-to-metal coupling boundary (Material Bridge)</li>
  <li><strong>Tolerance:</strong> ±0.05 mm for secure press-fit and adhesive uniformity</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>3. Internal Cavity</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Sensor Clearance:</strong> Internal geometry must allow 35 mm brass piezo element to seat flush against base interface without edge loading</li>
  <li><strong>Shielding:</strong> Fully enclosed metallic cavity required for electrical and environmental isolation</li>
  <li><strong>Exit Port:</strong> 3.0 mm lateral cable exit with integrated strain relief geometry</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>4. Mass Requirements</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Target Housing Mass:</strong> ≥ 40 g</li>
</ul>

<p>
Minimum mass is required to stabilize mechanical response and suppress high-frequency transient artifacts through inertial damping.
</p>

<hr>

<h3>5. Design Constraint Statement</h3>

<p>
These specifications define the geometric and material constraints for generation of STEP or CAD outputs within the repository.
All derived models must preserve these constraints without deviation.
</p>

<p>
The geometry is not aesthetic. It is a functional expression of the system’s mechanical design requirements.
</p>
