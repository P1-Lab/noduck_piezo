<h2>material_specs.md</h2>

<h3>1. Objective</h3>

<p>
This document specifies the material requirements for the Sovereign Capsule housing. Material selection is a functional constraint required to provide mechanical inertia for deterministic signal transduction and suppression of high-frequency artifacts ("quack").
</p>

<hr>

<h3>2. Material Properties and Mass Calculations</h3>

<p>
The housing is defined as a cylindrical capsule with 38.0 mm outer diameter and 1.5 mm wall thickness.
</p>

<hr>

<h3>3. Selection Criteria</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Inertia Threshold:</strong> Minimum housing mass of 40 g required to function as a mechanical low-pass filter.</li>
  <li><strong>Acoustic Damping:</strong> Zinc preferred where suppression of chassis resonance is required.</li>
  <li><strong>Structural Rigidity:</strong> Brass selected for high-stress environments requiring maximum mechanical coupling.</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>4. Boundary Enforcement</h3>

<p>
Regardless of material selection, the capsule base must be bonded to a wooden disc (Baltic Birch or Mahogany).
This establishes the mandatory Material Bridge between housing mass and the 35 mm brass piezo sensor.
</p>

<hr>

<h3>5. Regulatory Compliance</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Finish:</strong> Materials must remain unfinished or be electroplated with conductive coating to ensure Faradaic continuity.</li>
  <li><strong>Sourcing:</strong> All materials must be verified for structural integrity to preserve deterministic behavior of the 48 Hz resonant anchor.</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<p>
<strong>Constraint Statement:</strong><br>
Material selection is not aesthetic. It is a functional requirement of the Architecture of Necessity.
</p>
