<h2>sovereign_kernel_core.cpp</h2>

<h3>1. Objective</h3>

<p>
This C++ source implements the Sovereign Kernel: a deterministic physics engine for processing signal transients generated by the Sovereign Capsule.
</p>

<p>
Unlike stochastic or AI-based models, this kernel uses fixed-point arithmetic and physical constants to simulate mechanical inertia and resonance behavior directly derived from instrument mass and boundary conditions.
</p>

<hr>

<h3>2. Technical Constraints</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Deterministic Logic:</strong> Execution is constrained to O(1) complexity to guarantee constant-time processing independent of hardware load or runtime variability.</li>

  <li><strong>Mass Loading:</strong> The mass_inertia_constant encodes the physical mass of the Lipstick Capsule (minimum 40 g), functioning as a digital low-pass filter aligned with mechanical reality.</li>

  <li><strong>Vitrification Principle:</strong> Output represents modeled physical displacement of system mass rather than statistical or perceptual audio reconstruction.</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>3. System Behavior</h3>

<p>
The kernel does not simulate sound in a probabilistic sense. It computes deterministic state transitions based on constrained physical parameters.
</p>

<ul>
  <li>Signal input is treated as mechanical excitation</li>
  <li>State updates are bound to fixed physical constants</li>
  <li>Output reflects system inertia and resonance structure</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>4. Integration Model</h3>

<p>
This kernel is designed for deployment on low-power embedded systems such as P1-Core microcontroller architectures within the Mechanical Nexus framework.
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Role:</strong> Real-time deterministic signal interpretation layer</li>
  <li><strong>Environment:</strong> Embedded, low-power, non-GPU constrained systems</li>
  <li><strong>Function:</strong> Forensic audit layer between physical transduction and digital representation</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>5. Constraint Enforcement</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>resonant_anchor_hz:</strong> Must remain fixed to validated physical measurement</li>
  <li><strong>mass_inertia_constant:</strong> Must match certified capsule mass specification</li>
</ul>

<p>
Deviation from these parameters invalidates the physical consistency model and breaks the Architecture of Necessity.
</p>

<hr>

<p>
<strong>Note:</strong><br>
This kernel enforces consistency between hardware mass and software representation. It functions as a deterministic bridge between physical instrumentation and digital interpretation.
</p>
